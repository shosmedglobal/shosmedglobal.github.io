# Charles University Entrance Exam — What to Expect & How to Prepare

## Exam Format at a Glance

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Written Test** | 60 multiple-choice questions (30 Biology + 30 Chemistry) |
| **Format** | Online, taken on your own laptop via a browser-based platform |
| **Time Limit** | 2 hours for the written test |
| **Scoring** | 1 point per correct answer, no negative points for wrong answers |
| **Answer Format** | 4 options per question, exactly 1 correct |
| **Oral Interview** | 10 minutes, scored 12–62 points |
| **Interview Day** | Same day as the written exam |
| **Admitted per year** | Approximately 90 students |

---

## What You Need on Exam Day

- A computer or laptop with **Google Chrome** installed (recommended browser)
- A **working webcam** and **microphone** (you will be monitored via Zoom throughout)
- A **stable internet connection** — wired connection is strongly recommended
- Your **passport or national ID card**
- Optionally: a **printed periodic table** with atomic weights (this is allowed)

**You may NOT use**: notes, textbooks, additional devices, phones, or ask anyone for help during the exam.

---

## Before the Exam

1. You will receive access to a testing platform link
2. Log in and complete the **Practice Test** (4 sample questions) — this is mandatory to confirm you have no technical issues
3. The practice test does not count toward your score, but not completing it may cause procedural complications
4. Familiarize yourself with the interface: you can navigate between questions and change answers until time runs out

---

## During the Written Exam

- Log into the testing platform **15 minutes before** the scheduled start time
- Also connect to the **Zoom meeting** using the provided link — allow camera and microphone access
- You will be observed via your webcam for the entire duration of the exam
- You have **2 hours** to answer all 60 questions
- You can go back and change answers at any time before the timer expires
- The test will auto-submit when 2 hours are up
- **Any attempt at cheating will result in automatic failure** and inability to enroll

### Strategy Tips for the Written Test
- **Answer every question** — there are no negative points, so never leave a blank
- Budget roughly **2 minutes per question**, leaving time to review at the end
- Mark questions you're unsure about and come back to them
- Read each question carefully — look for keywords like "NOT," "EXCEPT," or "always"
- For chemistry, keep your periodic table handy for atomic masses and element properties
- For biology, pay close attention to whether questions ask about structure vs. function

---

## After the Written Exam

- Within **30 minutes** of the last candidate finishing, you will be told whether you passed the written portion
- If you pass, the **oral interview takes place the same day**
- The passing threshold is determined by the Dean based on expected admissions — it varies each year

---

## The Oral Interview

The interview lasts **10 minutes** and takes place via Zoom. It has two components:

### Part 1 — Text Analysis
You will receive a popular-scientific text to read and discuss. You are evaluated on:
- How much information you extract from the text
- How logically and concisely you present your ideas
- Your ability to synthesize and connect the text to broader knowledge
- Your ability to engage in discussion about the text
- The quality and clarity of your English

### Part 2 — Personal Interview
A broader assessment of your character, motivation, and fitness for medical school:
- Confidence, openness, and directness
- Interpersonal sensitivity and empathy
- Task orientation and focus
- Ability to handle ambiguity
- Critical thinking and reasoning
- Genuine motivation to study medicine

The evaluator also provides a personal recommendation score (1–7 points) based on their overall impression.

**Total interview score range: 12–62 points**

## Final Admission

- The official admission decision is sent after you submit your high school leaving certificate
- The required knowledge level corresponds to the Czech Republic's grammar school curriculum

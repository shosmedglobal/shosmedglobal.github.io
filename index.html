USMLE Pathway

From European Med School to US Residency

The #1 question every student asks: "Can I practice in the US after studying in Europe?" The answer is yes. Here's how.

1

Years 1-3: Pre-Clinical Studies

Focus on your medical curriculum. Begin USMLE Step 1 preparation alongside your coursework - the overlap is significant.

2

Year 3-4: USMLE Step 1

Take USMLE Step 1 (now pass/fail). Your European basic science training provides a strong foundation.

3

Years 4-5: Clinical Rotations + Step 2 CK

Complete clinical rotations in Prague. Prepare for and take Step 2 CK - this score matters most for your residency application.

4

Year 5-6: US Clinical Experience

Arrange US clinical electives/observerships to strengthen your application. We help you find and secure these opportunities.

5

ECFMG Certification + ERAS Application

Obtain ECFMG certification. Apply through ERAS to US residency programs. Our mentors guide you through every step.

✓

Match Day

Match into a US residency program and begin your career as a physician in the United States.

Our founder completed this exact pathway - from Charles University in Prague to Internal Medicine residency, and now a Hematology/Oncology fellowship at a major US academic medical center.

For Parents

Your Child's Medical Career, Made Clear

We know this is a big decision. Here are the facts parents ask about most.

Is Prague safe?

Prague is ranked among the safest cities in Europe, with a lower crime rate than most major US cities. Students thrive in its walkable, vibrant environment.

Will my child's degree be recognized in the US?

Yes. Charles University is fully recognized by ECFMG and meets WFME accreditation standards. Graduates are eligible to apply for US residency programs.

What about student loans?

US Federal Student Loans may be available depending on the faculty. Total costs are significantly lower than US or Caribbean medical schools even without loans.

How do graduates match into US residencies?

Through the same USMLE and ERAS process as any medical graduate. Our mentorship program provides step-by-step guidance through the entire process.

Admissions

How to Apply to LF3

A complete overview of the application process for the 2026/2027 academic year. No surprises — here's exactly what to expect.

1

Submit Your Application

Complete the online application on the Charles University portal and pay the admission fee of CZK 940 (approximately $40 USD). This is an official university fee.

2

Entrance Exam or Exam-Free Admission

Sit the LF3 entrance exam (Biology + Chemistry), or qualify for exam-free admission based on your academic record. See details below.

3

Submit Required Documents

Provide a verified high school certificate with apostille, official transcript, and a copy of your passport. All documents must be officially translated if not in Czech or English.

4

Certificate Recognition (Nostrification)

Your high school diploma must be recognized by Czech authorities. This process, called nostrification, has an official fee of CZK 990 (approximately $43 USD). We help you navigate this step.

Entrance Exam Details

  • Format: Online exam from your own computer (webcam + microphone required)
  • Written part: 60 multiple-choice questions — 30 Biology + 30 Chemistry — 2 hours total
  • Scoring: No negative marking. One correct answer per question.
  • Oral part: A short interview follows the written exam. Interview preparation is included in our Full Application Package.
  • Total score: 0–62 points (60 written + up to 2 from interview)

Exam-Free Admission

Up to 25 students per year may be admitted without taking the entrance exam, based on outstanding academic results. You must apply by April 15, 2026.

After You're Admitted

Tuition

420,000 CZK per year (approximately $18,500 USD). Payment by bank transfer only (no cash). Installment plans available on request.

Program

6-year General Medicine leading to an MUDr. degree (equivalent to MD). The program has been taught in English since 1991 and is accredited by the U.S. Department of Education for US student enrollment.

Language

Fully English-taught. Czech language proficiency is required by the end of year 2 so you can communicate with patients and staff during clinical rotations.

Class Size

Small, personalized classes with strong faculty-to-student ratios

Health Insurance

EU students: Bring your European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) for urgent care. For full coverage, register with a Czech health fund (VZP or ZPMV recommended - both offer free auxiliary insurance for foreign students). Non-EU students: Must have private health insurance covering the Czech Republic, or purchase insurance from VZP upon arrival.

Visa & Residence Permit

Non-EU students need a long-term visa or student residence permit. Start your application as early as possible - ideally by June. An official admission letter will be provided for your visa application. Processing times vary by country.

Required Documents for Enrollment

You'll need: verified high school leaving certificate (with apostille or super-legalization depending on your country), official transcript with all grades, and a passport copy. These must be sent to the Study Division before enrollment.

Certificate Recognition (Nostrification)

Your high school diploma must be recognized in the Czech Republic. You can do this through the university (CZK 990 fee, handled during application) or obtain a general nostrification from the Czech Ministry of Education separately.

Vaccinations

Hepatitis B vaccination is required (unless you can prove immunity). Measles vaccination documentation is also required. TB testing is available through the faculty.

Enrollment

Enrollment takes place in September, with the latest possible date being October 20. You must have all documents submitted and your visa arranged before you can enroll.

Why Czech Republic Over Caribbean?

Skip 4 Years of College

Caribbean and US med schools require a bachelor's degree first - that's 4 extra years and $100K-$200K before you even start med school.

EU-Recognized Degree

After graduating from LF3 at Charles University, you can also practice medicine in Europe if you choose — giving you career flexibility across two continents.

Fraction of the Cost

When you add college tuition to Caribbean med school, you're looking at $200K-$350K total. Charles University: ~$111K for the entire degree.

Live in Europe

Study in Prague — one of Europe's most vibrant, safe, and affordable capital cities, with world-class hospitals and clinical training sites throughout the city.

Globally Recognized

Charles University is a top-ranked, centuries-old institution - not a for-profit school with uncertain accreditation.

No MCAT Required

Skip the MCAT entirely. Charles University has its own entrance exam focused on biology, chemistry, and physics — no standardized US testing needed.

For Parents

Your Child's Medical Career, Made Clear

We know this is a big decision. Here are the facts parents ask about most.

Is Prague safe?

Prague is ranked among the safest cities in Europe, with a lower crime rate than most major US cities. Students thrive in its walkable, vibrant environment.

Will my child's degree be recognized in the US?

Yes. Charles University is fully recognized by ECFMG and meets WFME accreditation standards. Graduates are eligible to apply for US residency programs.

What about student loans?

US Federal Student Loans may be available depending on the faculty. Total costs are significantly lower than US or Caribbean medical schools even without loans.

How do graduates match into US residencies?

Through the same USMLE and ERAS process as any medical graduate. Our mentorship program provides step-by-step guidance through the entire process.

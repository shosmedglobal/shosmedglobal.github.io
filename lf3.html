US Pathway

Can you practice in the US after LF3?

Yes. Same residency match. Same MD license. Here is exactly how the IMG pathway works.

1

Earn your MD at LF3

A 6-year MUDr. degree (equivalent to MD), English-taught, recognized by ECFMG.

2

Take the USMLE

Step 1 (pass/fail), Step 2 CK (scored), and Step 3. Same exams every US medical graduate takes.

3

Get ECFMG-certified

The Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates verifies your credentials and clears you for US residency. ECFMG certification is the gateway every IMG goes through.

4

Match into US residency

Apply through ERAS, interview at programs, match through NRMP. The same system US graduates use. Thousands of European medical graduates match into US residencies.

Why LF3 Specifically

What you get at LF3 (and not at most alternatives)

Charles University has three medical faculties. LF3 is the one that combines modern clinical exposure with strong international reach.

Three decades of English instruction

English-taught MD program since 1991. You are not a beta student.

Real patients from Year 1

Classes are taught inside Kralovske Vinohrady University Hospital. You see wards early, not just lecture halls.

Practice across all 27 EU countries

Your diploma is automatically recognized under EU mutual recognition directives. No additional equivalency exams.

Direct line to US residency

ECFMG-recognized. Eligible for USMLE and the standard US residency match. Both of our co-founders did exactly this.

Small enough that faculty know your name

Intimate English-track cohort. Easier access to research, mentors, and a real alumni network.

One degree, two continents of options

Campus

Inside Kralovske Vinohrady University Hospital

Prague, Czech Republic
Fakultni Nemocnice
Kralovske Vinohrady
University Hospital · Vinohrady District
See on Google Maps →

LF3 is unusual among European medical faculties in being located directly inside a large teaching hospital. You learn anatomy, pathology, and clinical medicine in the same building where patients are being treated. By the end of Year 2, most students are already rotating through wards. Graduates consistently say this integrated-hospital model is the single biggest reason they felt ready for real clinical work by Year 5.

The hospital sits in Prague's Vinohrady district: central, walkable, well-connected by metro and trams. Student housing, grocery stores, cafes, and the main university buildings are all within 10 to 20 minutes.

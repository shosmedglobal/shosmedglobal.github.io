US Pathway

Can you practice in the US after LF3?

Yes. Same residency match. Same MD license. Here is exactly how the IMG pathway works.

1

Earn your MD at LF3

A 6-year MUDr. degree (equivalent to MD), English-taught, recognized by ECFMG.

2

Take the USMLE

Step 1 (pass/fail), Step 2 CK (scored), and Step 3. Same exams every US medical graduate takes.

3

Get ECFMG-certified

The Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates verifies your credentials and clears you for US residency. ECFMG certification is the gateway every IMG goes through.

4

Match into US residency

Apply through ERAS, interview at programs, match through NRMP. The same system US graduates use. Thousands of European medical graduates match into US residencies.

The Experience

Hospital-based from day one

LF3 is unusual: the faculty sits inside Fakultni Nemocnice Kralovske Vinohrady, a fully operational university hospital. Anatomy, pathology, and clinical medicine are taught in the same building where patients are being treated.

By Year 2, most students are already rotating through wards. Graduates consistently point to this hospital-embedded model as the reason they felt ready for real clinical work by Year 5.

Life in Prague

Life outside the hospital

Prague is one of the most livable student cities in Europe: safe, walkable, affordable by Western standards.

Vinohrady district

Central Prague, leafy and historic. Cafes, parks, and grocery stores within a 10-minute walk of the hospital.

20-minute commute, end-to-end

Three metro lines, dense tram network. Most students never own a car. A monthly transit pass is roughly $20.

A real student city

Large international community, English widely spoken in the center, low cost of living, direct trains to Berlin, Vienna, and Munich on weekends.

Campus address: Ruská 2411, 100 00 Praha 10-Vinohrady, Czechia. View on map.

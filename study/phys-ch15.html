Chapter 15

Harmonic Oscillation

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Simple harmonic motion is periodic motion where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement from equilibrium and directed toward it:

F = -k · x

This produces sinusoidal motion. The key equations for SHM:

QuantityFormula
Displacementy = A · sin(ω · t)
Velocityv = A · ω · cos(ω · t)
Accelerationa = -ω2 · y
Maximum velocityv_max = A · ω
Maximum accelerationa_max = A · ω2

Where:

  • A = amplitude (maximum displacement from equilibrium)
  • ω = angular frequency (in rad/s)
  • T = period (time for one complete oscillation)
  • f = frequency (oscillations per second, in Hz)

Relationships: ω = 2 · π · f = 2 · π / T

Spring-Mass System

A mass m attached to a spring with spring constant k oscillates with:

  • ω = √(k / m)
  • T = 2 · π · √(m / k)
  • f = (1 / (2 · π)) · √(k / m)

Important: The period depends on mass and spring constant only. It does NOT depend on amplitude (for ideal springs).

Simple Pendulum

A simple pendulum (point mass on a massless string of length L) oscillates with:

  • T = 2 · π · √(L / g)
  • f = (1 / (2 · π)) · √(g / L)

Important: The period depends on length and gravitational acceleration only. It does NOT depend on mass or amplitude (for small angles).

Energy in SHM

In SHM, energy continuously converts between kinetic and potential forms, but the total mechanical energy is constant:

E_total = KE + PE = (1/2) · k · A2 = constant

At any displacement y:

  • KE = (1/2) · k · (A2 - y2)
  • PE = (1/2) · k · y2
PositionKEPESpeed
Equilibrium (y = 0)MaximumZeroMaximum
Extremes (y = ±A)ZeroMaximumZero

Worked Example

Problem: A 0.5 kg mass is attached to a spring with k = 200 N/m. It is pulled 0.1 m from equilibrium and released. Find: (a) the period, (b) the maximum velocity, (c) the maximum acceleration.

Solution:

(a) T = 2 · π · √(m / k) = 2 · 3.14 · √(0.5 / 200) = 6.28 · √(0.0025) = 6.28 · 0.05 = 0.314 s

(b) First find ω: ω = 2 · π / T = 6.28 / 0.314 = 20 rad/s

v_max = A · ω = 0.1 · 20 = 2 m/s

(c) a_max = A · ω2 = 0.1 · 400 = 40 m/s2

Check: Total energy = (1/2) · k · A2 = (1/2) · 200 · 0.01 = 1 J. Max KE = (1/2) · m · v_max2 = (1/2) · 0.5 · 4 = 1 J. Consistent.

Exam Tips:
- Pendulum period depends on L and g only. Spring period depends on m and k only.
- Neither period depends on amplitude (for small oscillations).
- At equilibrium: max speed, zero acceleration. At extremes: zero speed, max acceleration.
- Doubling the amplitude doubles v_max and a_max, and quadruples the total energy.
- If you cut a spring in half, k doubles (shorter spring is stiffer).

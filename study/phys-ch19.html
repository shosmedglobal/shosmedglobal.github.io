Theory of Relativity (Basics)

Einstein's Special Relativity

Einstein's special theory of relativity (1905) is based on two postulates:

  1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames.
  2. The speed of light in vacuum (c = 3 · 108 m/s) is the same for all observers, regardless of their motion or the motion of the light source.

γ is always ≥ 1 and approaches infinity as v approaches c.

Time Dilation

A moving clock runs slower as seen by a stationary observer:

t = γ · t0 = t0 / √(1 - v2/c2)

Where t0 is the proper time (time measured by a clock at rest relative to the event) and t is the time measured by the stationary observer. Since γ > 1, the moving clock appears to tick slower (t > t0).

Length Contraction

A moving object appears shorter in the direction of motion:

L = L0 / γ = L0 · √(1 - v2/c2)

Where L0 is the proper length (measured in the object's rest frame). Since γ > 1, the moving object appears shorter (L < L0).

Mass-Energy Equivalence

E = m · c2

This is perhaps the most famous equation in physics. Rest mass and energy are equivalent. A small amount of mass corresponds to an enormous amount of energy because c2 is so large (9 · 1016 m2/s2).

  • Rest energy: E0 = m0 · c2
  • Total energy of a moving particle: E = γ · m0 · c2
  • Kinetic energy: KE = (γ - 1) · m0 · c2

Relativistic Addition of Velocities

If object A moves at velocity u relative to frame S, and frame S moves at velocity v relative to frame S', then the velocity of A in frame S' is:

u' = (u + v) / (1 + u·v/c2)

This ensures that no combined velocity ever exceeds c. For everyday speeds (u, v << c), the denominator is approximately 1, and this reduces to the familiar u' = u + v.

Worked Example

Problem: A spaceship travels at 0.8c relative to Earth. A clock on the spaceship measures a journey time of 10 years. How long does the journey take as measured by an observer on Earth?

Solution:

The proper time is t0 = 10 years (the spaceship clock is at rest relative to itself).

γ = 1 / √(1 - (0.8)2) = 1 / √(1 - 0.64) = 1 / √(0.36) = 1 / 0.6 = 5/3 = 1.667

t = γ · t0 = 1.667 · 10 = 16.67 years

The Earth observer measures 16.67 years, while the spaceship crew experiences only 10 years. This is real -- it has been confirmed with atomic clocks on aircraft and with GPS satellites.

Exam Tips:
- Time dilation: moving clocks run SLOW (longer time observed from outside).
- Length contraction: moving objects appear SHORTER in direction of motion.
- Effects only become significant at speeds comparable to c (above ~0.1c).
- Nothing with mass can reach or exceed c. Only massless particles (photons) travel at c.
- E = mc2 explains the enormous energy released in nuclear reactions.

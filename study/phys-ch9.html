Chapter 9

Magnetism

Magnetic Field

Magnetic field B, unit: tesla (T). Earth's field is roughly 5 x 10^-5 T.

Permeability of free space: mu_0 = 4*pi x 10^-7 T-m/A.

Force on a Moving Charge

F = q * v * B * sin(theta)

Direction: right-hand rule. For positive charges, point fingers along v, curl toward B, thumb gives F. For negative charges, reverse direction.

The magnetic force is always perpendicular to velocity => does NO work => changes direction only, not speed.

Radius of Circular Motion in B Field

r = m*v / (q*B)

A charged particle in a uniform B field moves in a circle.

Force Between Magnetic Poles

Historically, magnetic poles obey a Coulomb-like law. The force between two isolated magnetic poles of strengths p_1 and p_2 separated by distance r is:

F = (mu_0 / (4*pi)) * (p_1 * p_2) / r^2

In practice, magnetic poles always come in north-south pairs (no magnetic monopoles have been found). This formula is mostly of historical and theoretical interest. For exam purposes, know that the force between poles follows an inverse-square law, is attractive between unlike poles (N-S) and repulsive between like poles (N-N or S-S), and depends on the permeability of the medium between them.

Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

F = B * I * L * sin(theta)

Force Between Parallel Current-Carrying Conductors

Two long parallel wires carrying currents exert a magnetic force on each other. The force per unit length between them is:

F/L = (mu_0 * I_1 * I_2) / (2 * pi * d)

Where d is the distance between the wires.

  • Same direction currents: wires attract each other
  • Opposite direction currents: wires repel each other

Why? Each wire sits in the magnetic field created by the other wire. The force on wire 2 from the field of wire 1 is F = B_1 I_2 L, and B_1 = mu_0 * I_1 / (2*pi*d).

Worked Example: Two parallel wires 0.1 m apart each carry 10 A in the same direction. Force per meter?

  • F/L = (4*pi*10^-7 10 10) / (2 pi 0.1)
  • F/L = (4*pi*10^-5) / (0.2*pi) = 2 * 10^-4 N/m = 0.2 mN/m (attractive)

The SI Definition of the Ampere

The ampere was historically defined using this force: one ampere is the constant current which, if maintained in two straight parallel conductors of infinite length and negligible cross-section placed 1 meter apart in vacuum, produces a force of exactly 2 x 10^-7 newtons per meter of length between them.

(Since 2019, the ampere is redefined in terms of the elementary charge e = 1.602176634 x 10^-19 C, but the parallel-conductor definition remains important for exam purposes and gives physical intuition for the unit.)

Magnetic Field Around a Wire

B = mu_0 * I / (2*pi*r)

Direction: right-hand rule -- thumb along current, fingers curl in B direction.

Magnetic Field in a Solenoid

B = mu_0 * n * I

Where n = N/L (turns per unit length).

Magnetic Flux

Phi = B * A * cos(theta)

Unit: weber (Wb) = T*m^2. Theta is the angle between B and the normal to the surface.

Ampere's Rule (Right-Hand Rule for Solenoid)

Curl fingers in the direction of current flow around the solenoid; the thumb points toward the north pole (direction of B inside).

The Lorentz Force

The total electromagnetic force on a charged particle moving in both an electric field E and a magnetic field B is called the Lorentz force:

F = q*E + q*v x B

The electric field component (qE) acts along the field direction regardless of the particle's velocity. The magnetic component (qvB sin(theta)) is always perpendicular to the velocity.

When only B is present: The particle moves in a circle (if v is perpendicular to B) or a helix (if v has a component along B).

Radius of circular motion:

r = m*v / (q*B)

Period of circular orbit (cyclotron period):

T = 2*pi*m / (q*B)

A remarkable result: the period does NOT depend on the speed or the radius. Faster particles simply travel in larger circles but complete each revolution in the same time.

The Cyclotron

A cyclotron is a particle accelerator that uses this constant-period property. It consists of two hollow D-shaped electrodes ("dees") in a uniform magnetic field, with an alternating voltage applied across the gap between the dees.

How it works:

  1. A charged particle starts near the center.
  2. The magnetic field bends the particle in a semicircle inside one dee.
  3. When the particle crosses the gap between dees, the alternating voltage accelerates it.
  4. The particle enters the other dee at higher speed, traveling a larger semicircle (same period).
  5. This repeats, with the particle spiraling outward, gaining energy each time it crosses the gap.

Maximum kinetic energy:

KE_max = q^2 * B^2 * R^2 / (2*m)

Where R is the radius of the largest semicircle (the dee radius).

Worked Example: A proton (m = 1.67 x 10^-27 kg, q = 1.6 x 10^-19 C) in a cyclotron with B = 1.5 T. Find the cyclotron period.

  • T = 2*pi*m/(q*B) = 2(3.14)(1.67 x 10^-27) / (1.6 x 10^-19)(1.5)
  • T = 1.05 x 10^-26 / 2.4 x 10^-19 = 4.37 x 10^-8 s = 43.7 ns
Exam Tips:
- Magnetic force does NO work (perpendicular to velocity). Changes direction, not speed.
- Right-hand rule for POSITIVE charges. For electrons, reverse.
- A stationary charge in B experiences NO force. Must be moving.
- Electric fields accelerate (change speed). Magnetic fields deflect (change direction).
- In a cyclotron, the period is constant regardless of the particle's speed or orbit radius.
- The Lorentz force combines electric and magnetic forces. The magnetic part is always perpendicular to v.

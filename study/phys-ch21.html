Chapter 21

Faraday's Law of Electrolysis

What Is Electrolysis?

Electrolysis is the process of using electric current to drive a non-spontaneous chemical reaction. Ions in a solution or molten compound migrate to electrodes where they gain or lose electrons.

  • Cathode (negative electrode): reduction occurs (cations gain electrons)
  • Anode (positive electrode): oxidation occurs (anions lose electrons)

Faraday's First Law

The mass of substance deposited (or dissolved) at an electrode is directly proportional to the total charge passed:

m = (M · I · t) / (z · F)

Where:

  • m = mass deposited (in grams)
  • M = molar mass of the substance (g/mol)
  • I = current (in amperes)
  • t = time (in seconds)
  • z = number of electrons transferred per ion (valence number)
  • F = Faraday constant = 96485 C/mol (charge of one mole of electrons)

Faraday's Second Law

When the same quantity of charge is passed through different electrolytes, the masses of substances deposited are proportional to their electrochemical equivalents (M/z):

m_1 / m_2 = (M_1/z_1) / (M_2/z_2)

Worked Example

Problem: How many grams of copper are deposited when a current of 5 A flows through a CuSO_4 solution for 1 hour? (M_Cu = 63.5 g/mol, z = 2 for Cu2+)

Solution:

m = (M · I · t) / (z · F)

t = 1 hour = 3600 s

m = (63.5 · 5 · 3600) / (2 · 96485)

m = (63.5 · 18000) / 192970

m = 1143000 / 192970

m = 5.92 g

Check: Total charge Q = I · t = 5 · 3600 = 18000 C. Moles of electrons = 18000/96485 = 0.1866 mol. Since z = 2, moles of Cu = 0.1866/2 = 0.0933 mol. Mass = 0.0933 · 63.5 = 5.93 g. Consistent.

Exam Tips:
- Always convert time to seconds before calculating.
- z is the charge on the ion (Cu2+ has z = 2, Ag^+ has z = 1, Al3+ has z = 3).
- F = 96485 C/mol. For quick estimation, use F = 96500 C/mol.
- Total charge Q = I · t. This is the starting point for most electrolysis problems.
- Cathode = reduction (deposit metal). Anode = oxidation.

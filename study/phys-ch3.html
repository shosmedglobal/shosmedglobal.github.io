Dynamics (Newton's Laws) and Momentum

🧠Practice this chapter11 chapter-specific questions →

Newton's Three Laws

First Law (Inertia): An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion continues at constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

Second Law: Fnet = m · a. Direction of acceleration matches direction of net force.

More generally (impulse form): Fnet = dp/dt, where p is momentum.

Third Law: For every action force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force. These act on different objects.

Weight and Normal Force

Weight: W = m · g Normal (flat, no other vertical forces): N = m · g Normal (incline): N = m · g · cos(θ)

Friction

Static friction: fs ≤ μs · N (can be anything up to the max) Kinetic friction: fk = μk · N (fixed value)

Always: μs > μk. It takes more force to start motion than to maintain it.

Linear Momentum

p = m · v (unit: kg·m/s)

Impulse-Momentum Theorem

J = F · Δt = Δp = m·vf - m·vi

A longer contact time means a smaller average force for the same momentum change. This is why airbags, crumple zones, and bending your knees when landing all reduce impact force.

Conservation of Momentum

In an isolated system (no external forces):

m1·v1i + m2·v2i = m1·v1f + m2·v2f

Worked Example (Momentum): A 2 kg ball at 3 m/s collides with a 1 kg ball at rest, and they stick together.

  • pinitial = 2(3) + 1(0) = 6 kg·m/s
  • vf = 6/(2+1) = 2 m/s
  • KEinitial = (1/2)(2)(9) = 9 J
  • KEfinal = (1/2)(3)(4) = 6 J
  • Energy lost = 3 J (converted to heat/deformation)

Worked Example (Impulse): A 0.15 kg baseball at 40 m/s is hit by a bat and leaves at 50 m/s in the opposite direction. Contact time = 0.01 s.

  • Δp = 0.15(-50 - 40) = -13.5 kg·m/s
  • F = -13.5 / 0.01 = -1350 N (magnitude: 1350 N)

Worked Example (Newton): A 5 kg box on a floor, pushed with F = 30 N, μk = 0.2.

  • N = mg = 50 N, f = 0.2(50) = 10 N
  • Fnet = 30 - 10 = 20 N
  • a = 20/5 = 4 m/s2

Worked Example (Incline): A 10 kg box on a 30-degree ramp, μs = 0.6. Does it slide?

  • Fparallel = mg·sin(30) = 50 N
  • N = mg·cos(30) = 86.6 N
  • fs,max = 0.6(86.6) = 52 N > 50 N ⇒ box does NOT slide
Exam Tips:
- Third-law pairs act on DIFFERENT objects. They never cancel on a single object.
- On an incline, normal force = mg·cos(θ), NOT mg.
- Static friction is not always at maximum -- it adjusts to match applied force.
- In perfectly inelastic collisions, max KE is lost, but momentum is STILL conserved.

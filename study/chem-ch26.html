Chapter 26

Derivatives of Carboxylic Acids

Substitutional Derivatives

These are carboxylic acids where one or more hydrogens on the carbon chain have been replaced by another group.

Halogen Derivatives

Hydrogen atoms on the carbon chain are replaced by halogen atoms. The position matters: alpha-halo acids have the halogen on the carbon adjacent to COOH. Example: chloroacetic acid (ClCH₂COOH) is a stronger acid than acetic acid because the electronegative chlorine withdraws electron density, stabilizing the carboxylate anion.

Hydroxy Acids

Contain both –OH and –COOH groups. Biologically critical examples:

  • Lactic acid (2-hydroxypropanoic acid): Produced during anaerobic glycolysis in muscles. Has a chiral center.
  • Malic acid (2-hydroxybutanedioic acid): Citric acid cycle intermediate. Found in apples.
  • Citric acid (2-hydroxypropane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid): Central molecule of the citric acid cycle. Has three COOH groups and one OH.
  • Salicylic acid (2-hydroxybenzoic acid): Precursor of aspirin.

Oxo (Keto) Acids

Contain both a ketone (C=O) group and a carboxyl (COOH) group in the same molecule:

  • Pyruvic acid (2-oxopropanoic acid): End product of glycolysis. Connects glycolysis to the citric acid cycle.
  • Oxaloacetic acid (oxaloacetate, 3-oxobutanedioic acid): Citric acid cycle intermediate; combines with acetyl-CoA to form citrate.
  • 2-Oxoglutaric acid (alpha-ketoglutaric acid): Citric acid cycle intermediate; also involved in amino acid metabolism.

Amino Acids (Introduction)

Contain both an amino (–NH₂) group and a carboxyl group. These are the building blocks of proteins and will be covered in detail in Chapter 31.

Functional Derivatives

These are formed by modifying the –OH portion of the carboxyl group:

Reactivity order: Acyl halide > anhydride > ester > amide. The most reactive derivatives have the best leaving groups.

Key Points — Chapter 26

  • Substitutional derivatives: halogen acids, hydroxy acids (lactic, malic, citric, salicylic), oxo acids (pyruvic, oxaloacetic, 2-oxoglutaric).
  • Functional derivatives: salts, anhydrides, esters, amides, acyl halides, nitriles.
  • Esters form from acid + alcohol; amides from acid + amine; both lose water.
  • Reactivity: acyl halide > anhydride > ester > amide.
  • Pyruvic acid links glycolysis to the citric acid cycle.

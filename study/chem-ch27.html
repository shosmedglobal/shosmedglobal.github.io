Chapter 27

Sulfonic Acids and Carbonic Acid Derivatives

Sulfonic Acids

Sulfonic acids have the general formula R–SO₃H. They are formed by sulfonation (treating an organic compound with fuming sulfuric acid or SO₃). The sulfonic acid group is strongly acidic — fully ionized in water, making these strong acids comparable to mineral acids.

Aromatic sulfonic acids (like benzenesulfonic acid, C₆H₅SO₃H) are important in detergent chemistry. Sodium salts of long-chain alkylbenzenesulfonic acids are synthetic detergents (surfactants).

Biologically, sulfonic acids appear in taurine (2-aminoethanesulfonic acid), which is found in bile salts and plays a role in fat digestion.

Carbonic Acid and Its Derivatives

Carbonic acid (H₂CO₃) is the diprotic acid formed when CO₂ dissolves in water. It is extremely unstable and decomposes back to CO₂ and H₂O. However, its derivatives are stable and important:

Urea

(NH₂)₂CO

Urea is the diamide of carbonic acid. It is the primary end product of nitrogen metabolism in mammals — the liver converts toxic ammonia into urea, which is then excreted by the kidneys. Urea is highly water-soluble, non-toxic at physiological concentrations, and was the first organic compound synthesized from inorganic starting materials (by Friedrich Wohler in 1828, disproving vitalism).

Phosgene

COCl₂

Phosgene is the diacyl chloride of carbonic acid. It is an extremely toxic gas that was used as a chemical weapon. In industry, it is used to manufacture polycarbonate plastics and polyurethanes.

Guanidine

HN=C(NH₂)₂

Guanidine is a strong base. Its protonated form (guanidinium) is stabilized by resonance over three nitrogen atoms. Biologically, the guanidinium group is found in the amino acid arginine, giving it a positive charge at physiological pH. Creatine and creatinine also contain the guanidine moiety.

Key Points — Chapter 27

  • Sulfonic acids (R–SO₃H): strong organic acids; used in detergents.
  • Urea: diamide of carbonic acid; end product of nitrogen metabolism; excreted by kidneys.
  • Phosgene (COCl₂): toxic diacyl chloride of carbonic acid.
  • Guanidine: strong base; found in arginine, creatine, and creatinine.

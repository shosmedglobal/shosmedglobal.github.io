Kinematics

Kinematics describes motion without asking what causes it.

Position, Displacement, and Distance

Position is where an object is located relative to an origin.

Displacement is the change in position (a vector): Δx = xfinal - xinitial.

Distance is the total path length traveled (a scalar, always positive or zero).

Speed and Velocity

Average speed = total distance / total time (scalar, always ≥ 0).

Average velocity = displacement / time (vector, can be negative).

Instantaneous speed is speed at a single moment -- what a speedometer reads.

Uniform (Constant Velocity) Motion

When velocity is constant:

x = x0 + v·t

Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM)

When acceleration is constant, use these four equations:

(1) v = v0 + a·t (2) x = x0 + v0·t + (1/2)·a·t2 (3) v2 = v02 + 2·a·(x - x0) (4) x = x0 + (1/2)·(v0 + v)·t

Strategy: Each equation involves exactly four of the five unknowns (v0, v, a, t, x). Identify the three knowns and one unknown, then pick the equation that contains all four.

Free Fall

An object in free fall near Earth accelerates downward at g = 10 m/s2. Air resistance is neglected unless stated.

For an object dropped from rest:

v = g·t h = (1/2)·g·t2 v2 = 2·g·h

Worked Example 1: A ball is dropped from a 45 m tall building. How long to hit the ground?

  • h = (1/2)·g·t2 ⇒ 45 = 5·t2 ⇒ t2 = 9 ⇒ t = 3 s
  • Speed at impact: v = g·t = 10 x 3 = 30 m/s

Worked Example 2: A ball is thrown straight up at 20 m/s. Find the maximum height and total time in air.

  • At max height, v = 0: 0 = 202 - 2(10)·h ⇒ h = 20 m
  • Time up: 0 = 20 - 10·t ⇒ tup = 2 s
  • Total time (symmetric): ttotal = 4 s

Worked Example 3: An object is thrown downward at 5 m/s from a 60 m cliff. Find the time to reach the ground.

  • h = v0·t + (1/2)·g·t2 (taking downward as positive)
  • 60 = 5t + 5t2
  • 5t2 + 5t - 60 = 0 ⇒ t2 + t - 12 = 0
  • (t + 4)(t - 3) = 0 ⇒ t = 3 s (discard negative)

Projectile Motion

A projectile moves in two dimensions simultaneously:

  • Horizontal: constant velocity (no acceleration), so x = v0x · t
  • Vertical: free fall with a = g downward

For a projectile launched at angle θ with speed v0:

v0x = v0 · cos(θ) v0y = v0 · sin(θ) Time of flight: T = 2·v0·sin(θ) / g Maximum height: H = v02 · sin2(θ) / (2g) Range: R = v02 · sin(2·θ) / g

Key facts:

  • Maximum range occurs at θ = 45°.
  • Complementary angles (e.g., 30 and 60°) give equal range.
  • At the peak, the vertical velocity is zero but horizontal velocity is unchanged.

Worked Example 4: A ball is launched at 20 m/s at 30° above horizontal.

  • v0x = 20 · cos(30) = 17.3 m/s, v0y = 20 · sin(30) = 10 m/s
  • Time of flight: T = 2(10)/10 = 2 s
  • Range: R = 17.3 · 2 = 34.6 m
  • Max height: H = 100/20 = 5 m

Circular Motion

Period: T (time for one full revolution) Frequency: f = 1/T (in Hz) Angular velocity: ω = 2·π/T = 2·π·f (in rad/s) Speed: v = ω·r = 2·π·r/T Centripetal acceleration: ac = v2/r = ω2·r Centripetal force: Fc = m·v2/r = m·ω2·r

The centripetal acceleration always points toward the center of the circle.

Worked Example 5: A 0.5 kg ball on a 2 m string completes one revolution every 4 s.

  • ω = 2·π/T = 6.28/4 = 1.57 rad/s
  • ac = ω2 · r = (1.57)2 · 2 = 4.93 m/s2
  • Fc = m · ac = 0.5 · 4.93 = 2.47 N

Worked Example 6: A satellite orbits Earth at radius r with period T. If the radius is doubled, what happens to the period? (Kepler: T2 is proportional to r3)

  • T2 proportional to r3 ⇒ Tnew2 / T2 = (2r)3 / r3 = 8
  • Tnew = T · √(8) = 2·√(2)·T, approximately 2.83·T
Exam Tips:
- Free-fall acceleration is the same for all objects regardless of mass.
- At the highest point of a projectile, velocity is NOT zero -- only the vertical component is zero.
- Centripetal force is not a new type of force. It is provided by tension, gravity, friction, etc.
- When an object is thrown upward, acceleration is g downward the entire time -- even at the top.

Chapter 7

Gravitation

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

F = G * m_1 * m_2 / r^2

Where G = 6.67 x 10^-11 N*m^2/kg^2, r = distance between centers.

Always attractive, obeys inverse-square law.

Gravitational Field Strength

g = G * M / r^2

At Earth's surface: g = 9.8 m/s^2 (use 10).

At altitude h: g_h = G*M/(R+h)^2.

Worked Example: At what altitude is g reduced to 1/4 of surface value?

  • GM/(R+h)^2 = GM/(4R^2) => (R+h)^2 = 4R^2 => h = R

Gravitational Potential Energy (General)

PE = -G * m_1 * m_2 / r

The negative sign indicates a bound system. PE = 0 at infinite separation.

Escape Velocity

v_escape = sqrt(2*G*M/R) = sqrt(2*g*R)

For Earth: v_escape is approximately 11.2 km/s.

Orbital Velocity

v_orbital = sqrt(G*M/r)

For a circular orbit, gravitational force provides centripetal force.

Exam Tips:
- The r in the formula is CENTER to CENTER, not surface to surface.
- G (gravitational constant) and g (gravitational acceleration) are different quantities.
- Doubling both masses quadruples force. Doubling distance quarters force.
- Gravitational force is always attractive (unlike electric force).

Scalars vs Vectors

Scalars have magnitude only: mass, time, energy, temperature, speed, distance, work, power, charge, density, pressure, electric potential.

Vectors have both magnitude and direction: force, velocity, acceleration, displacement, momentum, electric field, magnetic field, torque.

Key distinction: Speed is scalar, velocity is vector. Distance is scalar, displacement is vector. A car driving 100 km around a track back to the start has distance = 100 km but displacement = 0.

Dimensional Analysis

Every valid physics equation must have the same dimensions on both sides. This technique lets you check whether a formula is plausible.

Worked Example: Verify that KE = (1/2)mv2 has units of energy.

  • [KE] = kg x (m/s)2 = kg·m2/s2
  • This equals the joule (J = N·m = kg·m/s2 x m = kg·m2/s2). Confirmed.

Worked Example: A student writes the period of a pendulum as T = 2·π·√(g/L). Check dimensions.

  • [g/L] = (m/s2) / m = 1/s2
  • √(1/s2) = 1/s, so T would have units of 1/s. But period should be in seconds.
  • The correct formula is T = 2·π·√(L/g), giving √(s2) = s. The student is wrong.

Worked Example: Which formula for drag force is dimensionally correct? (a) F = ρ·v·A or (b) F = (1/2)·ρ·v2·A

  • (a): [ρ·v·A] = (kg/m3)(m/s)(m2) = kg/(m·s). NOT force units.
  • (b): [ρ·v2·A] = (kg/m3)(m2/s2)(m2) = kg·m/s2 = N. This IS force. Answer: (b).
Exam Tips:
- Confusing mass (kg) with weight (N). Weight = mg, different units.
- The kilogram is the base unit of mass, not the gram.
- Kelvin has no degree symbol -- it is just "K", not "degrees K."
- When converting areas, square the conversion factor: 1 m2 = 104 cm2, not 102 cm2.
- Similarly for volume: 1 m3 = 106 cm3.
- When a problem gives values in mixed units (e.g., km and m), convert everything to SI before calculating.

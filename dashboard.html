Everything you need to know about applying to LF3 at Charles University.

Always verify with the official source. The figures and dates here reflect publicly available LF3 / Charles University data. Specific deadlines, tuition, fees, and document requirements can change between admission cycles. Confirm the current details on the official portal at is.cuni.cz.

LF3 Entrance Exam

The Third Faculty of Medicine (LF3) uses a combined written and oral format.

LF3 - Third Faculty of Medicine Prague

Subjects Biology (30 MCQ) + Chemistry (30 MCQ) = 60 questions
Format Electronic written test + 10-min oral interview
Admitted ~90 students per year

Required Documents

What LF3 needs from you, organized by stage. Apply through the Charles University portal at is.cuni.cz.

!
A high school diploma is required to begin studying at LF3. You must have completed secondary school and hold a high school diploma. International applicants must have their diploma nostrified (officially recognized in the Czech Republic). Nostrification can be completed after admission, before enrollment.

1 When you apply (online, before the deadline)

  • Completed online application form via is.cuni.cz
  • High school diploma (certified copy with English or Czech translation if originally issued in another language)
  • Official high school transcript of grades
  • Valid passport copy
  • Application fee payment confirmation (set by Charles University each year; recently around 940 CZK / approximately $40)

2 Before you enroll (after you receive your acceptance)

  • Nostrification of the high school diploma (Czech recognition of foreign education; can be done through Charles University or directly through the Czech Ministry of Education)
  • Student visa (non-EU applicants only) - submitted to the Czech embassy with admission letter, proof of accommodation, and health insurance
  • Health insurance valid in the Czech Republic (EU students: EHIC plus Czech health fund. Non-EU: comprehensive private plan or VZP)
  • Health certificate from a physician (required for enrollment)
  • Hepatitis B and measles vaccination records (required before clinical rotations begin in Year 2; arrange early)

Note: Document requirements are set by LF3, Charles University, and the Czech Ministry of the Interior, and may be updated between admission cycles. Always confirm the current list on the official portal before submitting.

After You're Admitted

Once you receive your LF3 acceptance letter, here are the logistics for your first months. (For the documents you need during this stage, see Required Documents above.)

💵 Tuition

Approximately 420,000 CZK / year (~$18,500 USD). Bank transfer to Charles University; installment plans usually available. Tuition is set annually by LF3, so verify the current rate at admission.

🎓 Program

6-year General Medicine, MUDr. degree (equivalent to MD). English-taught since 1991. ECFMG-recognized for US residency.

🗣️ Language

Czech proficiency required by end of Year 2 for clinical rotations. Basic Czech language coursework is included in the curriculum.

🛡️ Visa (non-EU)

Apply at the Czech embassy or consulate as soon as you have your admission letter. The process typically takes 6 to 10 weeks, so start early (by June for an October start).

📅 Enrollment

In-person enrollment takes place at LF3 in September. Studies begin October 1 (the start of the Czech academic year).

Note: Procedures and fees are set by LF3 and Czech authorities; details may change. Confirm current logistics with LF3 directly after admission.

Questions about any of these? Email us and we will walk you through it.

USMLE Timeline for IMGs

Recommended timeline for international medical graduates preparing for USMLE and residency match.

Years 1-3: Pre-Clinical

Focus on medical curriculum. Begin Step 1 study alongside coursework - the overlap with basic sciences is significant.

Year 3-4: USMLE Step 1

Take Step 1 (now pass/fail). Dedicate 3-6 months of focused prep. Resources: First Aid, UWorld, Pathoma, Sketchy.

Year 4-5: Step 2 CK + Clinical Experience

Score competitively on Step 2 CK - this is the most important score. Arrange US clinical rotations/observerships.

Year 5-6: ECFMG + Applications

Complete ECFMG certification via Pathways. Build your CV, personal statement. Secure strong LORs.

September: ERAS Opens

Submit ERAS application. Apply broadly as an IMG - 50-100+ programs depending on specialty.

October - January: Interview Season

Schedule and attend interviews. Prepare with mock interviews. Send thank-you notes.

March: Match Day

Submit rank list in February. Match results announced in March. Begin residency in July.

Tips for IMG Applicants

Practical advice from physicians who matched as IMGs.

🏥 US Clinical Experience

US clinical experience (observerships, externships, clinical rotations) is crucial for IMGs. It demonstrates familiarity with the US healthcare system and provides opportunities for strong LORs. Aim for 3-6 months of US experience, ideally in your target specialty.

🔬 Research

Research publications strengthen your application significantly. Even case reports or poster presentations count. Look for research opportunities through your medical school, US hospitals, or online collaborations. Aim for at least 1-3 publications or presentations.

📝 Letters of Recommendation

Strong LORs from US-based physicians carry the most weight. During your clinical rotations, build genuine relationships with attendings. Ask for letters well in advance (2-3 months). A letter from a department chair or program director in your specialty is especially valuable.

🌍 Visa Considerations

Most IMG residents train on J-1 or H-1B visas. J-1 is more common and easier to obtain through ECFMG sponsorship but requires a 2-year home country return (with waiver options). H-1B requires employer sponsorship. Filter programs on Residency Explorer by visa sponsorship availability.

🎯 Application Strategy

Apply broadly - IMGs should target 50-100+ programs depending on specialty competitiveness. Focus on IMG-friendly programs (check NRMP data for IMG match rates). Apply on day one when ERAS opens - early applications get more interview invitations. Use Residency Explorer and FREIDA to research programs.
